Enterprise Networks

For modern corporations, network security and data control are paramount. Our system provides a powerful tool to enhance both within a corporate LAN or WAN.

  • Internal Infrastructure Security: Creates a tamper-proof naming system for critical internal services, eliminating threats like internal DNS cache poisoning and providing a fully auditable record of all changes.
  • Complete Operational Sovereignty: Allows companies to manage their own private domains (e.g., `.mycorp`) without reliance on external bodies like ICANN, protecting them from global outages and external policy changes.

Collaborative Consortiums

When multiple independent organizations need to collaborate, establishing a shared, trusted digital infrastructure is a major challenge.

  • Trustless Collaboration: Enables groups like supply chains or research alliances to create a common, trusted naming service where no single member can unilaterally alter records, fostering high data integrity.
  • Stable Naming for Shared Resources: Simplifies integration between members' systems by providing stable, human-readable names for shared APIs and data, ensuring resilience even if one member's infrastructure fails.

Critical Infrastructure & IoT

For systems where downtime is not an option, the high availability and fault tolerance of our design have a profound impact.

  • Uninterrupted Service: In environments like smart grids or hospital networks, our system's automatic failover ensures the name resolution service remains operational 24/7, preventing catastrophic failures.
  • Secure Naming for IoT: Creates a secure, private naming network for thousands of IoT devices in environments like factories, preventing device hijacking through DNS manipulation.

Community Networks

While designed for permissioned networks, the underlying principles provide a powerful blueprint for censorship-resistant community networks.

  • Censorship Resistance: Activist groups, journalists, or communities in restrictive environments can deploy their own instance to create a naming infrastructure that is not subject to takedown orders from a central government or corporation.
  • Information Freedom: Ensures that the ability for a community to communicate and share information remains intact and under its own control.
